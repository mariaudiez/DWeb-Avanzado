About us

Our mision

THALÍA was born from the belief that painting remains a language of emotion and revelation.

Within Madrid’s Crystal Palace, twelve women artists turn gesture into poetry, color into thought.

The festival celebrates the strength and sensitivity of the feminine gaze in contemporary art.

THALÍA was conceived as an encounter between art, architecture, and the feminine spirit.

The festival takes its name from the Greek muse of creative expression, symbolizing inspiration and renewal.

Within the Crystal Palace of Madrid, twelve women painters transform the transparency of glass into a dialogue of gesture and emotion.

The concept is simple yet profound, to reveal how painting remains a living language, capable of expressing the invisible through color and presence.

At the heart of THALÍA lies a commitment to authenticity, equality, and beauty as consciousness.

The festival values the act of creation as a form of dialogue; between artist and canvas, light and matter, silence and voice.

By presenting only female painters, THALÍA reaffirms the importance of representation, offering visibility to diverse perspectives and re-centering the feminine gaze in the global art discourse.

Each work exhibited embodies respect, emotion, and the quiet power of sensitivity as strength.

THALÍA emerged in Madrid as an independent curatorial initiative, born from the desire to create a new space for contemporary painting led by women.

The choice of the Crystal Palace (a historic monument of glass and light) was not accidental: its architecture reflects the festival’s vision of transparency, openness, and transformation.

Since its creation, THALÍA has sought to connect local and international audiences through experiences that merge art and atmosphere.

It stands as both a celebration and a statement; that the future of art is plural, luminous, and profoundly human.
